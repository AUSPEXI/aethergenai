Evidence‑Led AI in Regulated Industries: A Practical Guide

If you work in a regulated organisation, you’ve felt the tension: the business wants AI yesterday, while compliance, privacy, and procurement demand proof. AethergenPlatform solves this by making evidence the default—every dataset/model ships with signed metrics, lineage, and privacy probes buyers can verify.

What “Evidence‑Led” Means

  • Lineage: schema, versions, recipe hashes, artifact checksums
  • Utility: task accuracy deltas vs baselines (AUC/KS/PR‑AUC)
  • Privacy: membership‑inference and attribute‑disclosure probes; optional DP (ε, δ)
  • Ablation: what changes outcomes and by how much (effect sizes)

The Privacy Bit (Plain Language)

We default to synthetic‑first: learn patterns from minimal/redacted samples, then generate new records that behave like the real thing without carrying identifiers. When needed, we apply differential privacy and publish probes to show acceptable risk.

A Concrete Example

Credit‑risk under Basel governance: we build a synthetic transaction graph with risk features and typology injection; train/evaluate; then ship a signed bundle covering utility, privacy, drift stability, and ablations. Reviewers get facts, not slideware.

How AethergenPlatform Implements This

  1. Schema Designer: define fields, constraints, privacy levels
  2. Generator: produce synthetic datasets at the volumes you need with ε control
  3. Benchmarks & Ablation: run task evaluations, stress, and ablations
  4. Reporting: export a signed evidence bundle and model/dataset cards

Why We’re Different

Evidence is a first‑class artifact—automated in CI and independently verifiable. We protect IP while proving claims (optional zk‑proofs for privacy bounds), so adoption moves fast without blind trust.
