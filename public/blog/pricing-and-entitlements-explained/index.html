Pricing & Entitlements Explained: Self‑Service vs Full‑Service

By Gwylym Owen — 16–22 min read

Executive Summary

AethergenPlatform offers transparent tiers that map to real‑world needs while avoiding cannibalisation. Compute ownership stays with customers; we price for rights, evidence, and support.

Principles

  • Evidence‑first: every claim backed by signed metrics.
  • Rights‑based pricing: you pay for usage rights and support, not surprise compute.
  • Non‑cannibalising tiers: each tier has a clear reason to exist.

Tiers

  • Self‑Service: access to packaged assets, docs, and community support.
  • Assisted: guided setup, limited customization, priority support.
  • Full‑Service: private listings, custom adapters, SLAs, roadmap input.

Entitlements

  • Asset rights (trial, evaluation, production).
  • Support response windows (best‑effort → SLA).
  • Customization (none → limited → bespoke).
  • Update cadence (regular → priority → negotiated).

Compute Ownership

Customers own compute for training/inference. We publish expected costs so budgeting is predictable; we focus pricing on rights and service, not hidden cycles.

Evidence Alignment

  • Operating points matched to business capacity.
  • Stability and drift thresholds set per deployment.
  • Evidence bundles attached to change‑control.

Anti‑Cannibalisation Design

  • Each tier unlocks qualitatively different outcomes.
  • Higher tiers include governance and risk reductions, not just features.
  • Clear upgrade paths without discounting lower tiers’ value.

Billing Scenarios

  • Dataset subscription (monthly/annual) with updates.
  • Model subscription with support and evidence refresh.
  • Project‑based full‑service engagements with milestones.

FAQ

Do you charge for compute?

No—we design for your infra; our pricing is rights and service.

What happens if we outgrow a tier?

Upgrade paths are contractual; we keep evidence continuity across tiers.

Can we do pilots?

Yes—time‑limited pilots with acceptance gates and rollback plans.

Glossary

  • Entitlement: the rights a customer has to use assets/services.
  • Operating point: threshold where a model is evaluated/used.
  • Evidence bundle: signed metrics/configs/hashes.

Checklist
